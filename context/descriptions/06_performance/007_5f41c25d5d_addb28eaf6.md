# 007 — PER Group Cache + RowRange in Quadratic Builder + VarResolver Hot Loops

**Commit**: `addb28eaf6` (`decide: PER group cache + RowRange in quadratic builder + VarResolver hot loops`)
**Baseline**: `5f41c25d5d`
**Benchmark JSONs**: `benchmark/decide/results/addb28eaf6.json` (this commit), `benchmark/decide/results/5f41c25d5d.json` (baseline)
**Suite**: `benchmark/decide/run_benchmarks.py`, sizes `medium` + `large`, 3 iterations each.

## Changes

Three model-building speedups landed in one commit, planned as TODO items #4, #5, #6 from `context/descriptions/todo.md`. Plan: `~/.claude/plans/lets-do-items-4-playful-curry.md`.

1. **PER group cache (Item 4).** `BuildGroupIds()` previously ran a full `ColumnDataCollection` scan + hash-collision-tolerant group-map build for every constraint and the objective independently. When the same PER expression set shows up in multiple constraints (or constraint + objective), all that work was redone. New `PerGroupCache` (`std::unordered_map<size_t, vector<PerGroupCacheEntry>>`) lives for the duration of one `Finalize` and stores the *unfiltered* row→group mapping keyed by `(per_columns, null_excludes)`. Hash key is xor-fold of `Expression::Hash()`; equality is pairwise `Expression::Equals()`. Each call site applies its own WHEN/local filter as a post-pass and remaps surviving group IDs to consecutive `0..K'` so downstream code sees the same dense `[0..K')` range as before — no semantic change.
2. **RowRange in `BuildQuadraticConstraint` (Item 5).** The lambda took a `const vector<idx_t> &row_set`. Three callers materialized that vector freshly: ungrouped used `vector<idx_t>(num_rows); std::iota(...)`, grouped sliced `vector<idx_t>(flat_rows.begin()+g_begin, flat_rows.begin()+g_end)` per group, per-row built `vector<idx_t>{row}`. New `RowRange { rows*, count, dense_iota }` lets all three callers pass either a CSR slice pointer or a "dense iota" marker without allocating. The lambda's four inner row iterations (linear, bilinear, power-group inner, power-group outer) now go via `range.Get(i)`.
3. **`VarResolver` for hot inner loops (Item 6).** Every coefficient contribution called `VarIndexer::Get(var_idx, row)`, which branches on `is_entity_scoped[var_idx]` then for entity-scoped vars dereferences `entity_mappings_ref`, indexes `mappings[var_entity_mapping_idx[var_idx]]`, and looks up `row_to_entity[row]` — a chain of vector lookups per call. New `VarResolver` (`{entity_flag, row_var_offset, num_row_vars, entity_base, row_to_entity*}`) is built once per (variable, indexer) outside the row loop. `resolver.Get(row)` is then one branch + array-index for entity-scoped, or one multiply-add for row-scoped. Adopted at all 23 hot `Get()` sites: 13 in `ilp_model_builder.cpp` (objective linear, quadratic POWER, bilinear, aggregate fast/slow path with and without groups, per-row constraints, quadratic constraint terms) and 10 in `physical_decide.cpp` (deferred-NE, PER objective inner aggregate easy/hard, flat MIN/MAX easy/hard, composed MIN/MAX constraint and objective, plus 5 readback type branches). The plain `Get(var_idx, row)` is retained for entity-mapping setup and any sites that need to tolerate `INVALID_INDEX`.

## Hypothesis

- **Item 4 is the headliner.** Q4 (`nested_per_objective`) reuses the same PER spec (`l_returnflag`) across two constraints + the objective — three independent BuildGroupIds invocations on a 500K/1M row table. Eliminating two of those should cut model-build time visibly.
- **Items 5 & 6 are micro-optimizations.** Solver dominates ~95% of wall time on every benchmark query, and model-build is small in absolute terms (5–500ms). Even if RowRange and VarResolver halve the inner-loop overhead, the absolute savings are below the run-to-run noise floor on this bench.

## Reading the results

- **Q4 -50% on `model_construction_ms`** is exactly what Item 4 was designed for. Q4's three PER-using sites (`SUM(x) PER l_returnflag`, `SUM(x*l_quantity) PER l_returnflag`, and `MAX(SUM(x*l_extendedprice)) PER l_returnflag`) all share the same PER expression. Pre-cache: three full scans + three hash-collision-tolerant group-map builds. Post-cache: one scan + one map build, two cheap filter+remap passes. Both medium and large halve in lockstep, confirming the cache hit rate is data-size-independent (which it should be — number of cache lookups depends on number of PER call sites, not row count).
- **Q7 model-build flat at -1.7%/-0.5%.** Q7 (`entity_scope_join`) was a candidate for Item 6 since it has the heaviest entity-scoped variable workload. The savings *exist* but are below the noise floor. Q7's model-build is dominated by entity-mapping construction in `BuildGroupIds` (already streamed in commit 5f41c25d5d) and per-constraint coefficient evaluation in `EvaluateLinearExpression`, not by the `Get()` calls inside the model builder. The hot loops VarResolver targets are simply not the bottleneck on this bench.
- **Other queries flat (±1-2%).** Items 5/6 are real wins inside their hot loops, but model-build is too small a fraction of wall time on these queries for the savings to register.
- **Q9 large solver +36.4%** is run-to-run Gurobi non-determinism on the MIP B&B for this purely-quadratic objective. `model_construction_ms` essentially flat (+1.1%, ~0.15ms absolute). Q9 medium solver was -0.4%, ruling out a systematic regression. Same pattern as entries 005 and 006 — Q9's solver wall swings ±20-40% across runs.

- All 547 decide differential tests pass (`make decide-test`).
- Build clean (`make release`).
- Encounter-order remap in `LookupOrBuildPerGroupIds` preserves the legacy "groups labeled 0..K-1 in first-seen row order" semantics — covered transitively by every PER-using test in the suite, including the `RejectEmptyAggregate` aggregate-rejection paths.

## What this leaves on the table

- **Sparse-masked coefficient column variant (TODO item, kept).** The NE per-row indicator path still allocates a Dense column with mostly zeros; a SparseMasked variant would help that one path. Skipped for the same reason as before — broader-population mutation patterns force promotion-back-to-Dense.
- **True streaming-during-evaluation aggregates (TODO item, kept).** Items 4-6 didn't touch the per-row coefficient materialization; restructuring MIN/MAX, NE, ABS, McCormick to consume streaming chunks remains open.
- **PER cache by canonical expression form rather than `Hash`/`Equals`.** The current cache catches PER specs that are structurally identical at the Expression level. Two semantically identical specs that the binder produced as different Expression trees (different alias capture, different cast layering) would miss the cache. Not exercised by the bench; revisit if a workload shows up.
- **Items 5/6 wins not yet captured on this bench.** The optimizations are real but masked by solver dominance. They'd matter more on dense entity-scoped queries with millions of coefficient writes per constraint (workloads where model-build is >20% of wall). No such query in the current bench.
