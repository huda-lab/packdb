## Changes

Three localized speedups in `PhysicalDecide::Finalize()`, planned as TODO items #4, #5, #9 from `context/descriptions/todo.md`. Plan: `~/.claude/plans/plan-out-4-5-encapsulated-lark.md`.

1. **Per-Finalize chunk-expression cache.** `TransformToChunkExpression()` recursively rewrites a bound expression tree (swapping `BoundColumnRef` â†’ `BoundReferenceExpression`, plus copies of casts/comparisons/conjunctions/operators) every time it's called. Eleven distinct call sites in `Finalize` â€” WHEN conditions, constraint LHS coefficients, RHS, PER columns, bilinear coefficients, quadratic inner terms, objective coefficient buckets, objective bilinear terms, objective PER, and the two composed-MIN/MAX lambdas â€” each used to drive their own recursion. New `ChunkExprCache` (`std::unordered_map<const Expression *, unique_ptr<Expression>>`) lives for the duration of one `Finalize` and is shared across all call sites. `CachedTransformToChunkExpression` looks up by Expression pointer identity (stable across one Finalize) and returns a `const Expression &` to the cache-owned transformed copy. Per-call-site `vector<unique_ptr<Expression>>` storage was dropped; lifetime now belongs to the cache. `BuildGroupIds` signature changed to `vector<const Expression *>` so PER call sites can pass cached references directly.
2. **Streaming group mapping.** `BuildGroupIds()` previously materialized `vector<vector<Value>> key_columns(num_key_cols)` of size O(num_rows Ă— num_key_cols) Values up front, then walked rows post-scan to assign group IDs. Rewritten to assign group IDs *during* the chunk scan: representatives stored once per group (`rep_keys` of size O(num_groups Ă— num_key_cols)), null checks done via `UnifiedVectorFormat::validity` without allocating a `Value`, and `vec.GetValue(i)` only called on the (rare) hash-collision equality fallback path or when emitting a fresh representative. `KeyTuplesEqual` was deleted (no other callers).
3. **ABS upper-bound reserve.** The MAXIMIZE-ABS Big-M expansion previously copied 5 vectors out of each source `EvaluatedConstraint` (`c1_vis`/`c1_rcs`/`c1_rhs`/`c1_rgids`/`c1_ngrps` plus c2 mirrors) into local intermediates *because* `push_back` could reallocate `gstate.evaluated_constraints` and invalidate references. New code reserves `current_size + 2 * abs_maximize_links.size()` up front, then references C1/C2 directly â€” saves 10 vector deep-copies per ABS link.

## Hypothesis

- Item 1: targets queries with many constraints/objective terms that share coefficient/PER expression pointers. Composed MIN/MAX (Q4) was the obvious case â€” its `EvaluateTermCoefs` lambda is called once per term, each doing its own full data scan with its own transformation. Q7's entity-scope mapping plus per-constraint workload should see savings on RHS/coefficient transformations across many constraints.
- Item 2: helps queries that materialize entity mappings or PER groupings over large row counts with relatively few groups. Q7 (entity_scope_join, half-million rows Ă— few entities) is the canonical fit.
- Item 3: bounded payoff â€” only the MAXIMIZE-ABS path benefits, and the savings scale with `abs_maximize_links.size()`. Q2 (`abs_minmax`) is the only query exercising it. Expect small absolute improvement.

## Reading the results

- **Q4 -10â€“13% on model_construction_ms** is the headline. Q4 (`nested_per_objective`) exercises the composed-MIN/MAX path most heavily â€” `EvaluateTermCoefs`/`EvaluateTermCoefsObj` were each transforming + recursively copying a fresh expression tree per term. Pointer-identity caching pays off when terms reference the same coefficient subtree across the inner/outer composition.
- **Q7 -3.4â€“7.1% on model_construction_ms.** Q7's baseline is the heaviest of any query (~506 ms large, ~247 ms medium) because it has both entity-scope mapping (now streamed) and many constraints sharing RHS/PER expressions. The bigger relative win at medium suggests the entity-mapping streaming dominates: at medium size, the post-scan two-pass over `key_columns` was a bigger fraction of the model time.
- **Q1 medium -5.9%, large flat.** Q1 large is dominated by 500K-row coefficient evaluation; the cache helps less because Q1 has only one constraint with two terms. Medium shows the cache hits for repeated WHEN/RHS transformations more clearly.
- **Q2 slightly negative** (+0.8% to +1.4% model_ms). Q2 (`abs_minmax`) is the only query exercising the ABS reserve path, but its dominant cost is the per-row Big-M coefficient computation, not the vector copies. The cache adds a small lookup overhead per call site that's measurable here. Within run-to-run noise (Q2 medium: 27.98 â†’ 28.19 = +0.21ms, < 1% of variance).
- **Q9 wall time -16% to -17%** is solver noise, mirroring entry 005's +25% in the opposite direction. `model_construction_ms` flat (13.68 â†’ 13.70). Q9 is pure quadratic; Gurobi's MIP B&B dominates the 11â€“28s wall and routinely swings.
- **Q3, Q5, Q6, Q8 flat.** No path heavily exercised by the changes â€” single-constraint or simple objectives without composed expressions.

## What this leaves on the table

- **PER cache across constraints (TODO #6).** The chunk-expression cache catches the *transformation* of PER columns, but `BuildGroupIds` still does a full scan + group assignment per constraint. If multiple constraints share an identical PER column set, we could cache the resulting `row_group_ids` and group metadata too. Larger surface â€” touches both constraint and objective PER paths and needs cache invalidation logic.
- **`VarIndexer::Get()` specialization (TODO #8).** Still on the list. The branchy entity-scope check inside `Get()` is called from every coefficient contribution; a specialized flat-index iterator would avoid the per-call branch. Worth profiling first.
- **True streaming aggregates (deferred from #2).** `BuildGroupIds` now streams group assignment but the constraint/objective coefficient evaluation paths still materialize per-row coefficients before downstream MIN/MAX, NE, ABS, and McCormick consumers reach for them. Restructuring those derivations to consume streaming chunks remains open.
- **Cache key on canonical expression form, not pointer identity.** Pointer identity catches in-Finalize reuse (composed MIN/MAX terms, same constraint's term list) but misses semantically-identical-but-distinct Expression nodes across constraints. A canonical-string or structural hash would catch more, at the cost of a per-call hashing pass that may or may not pay back. Not measurably needed on the current bench.
